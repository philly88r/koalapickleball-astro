<h3 class="\&quot;wp-block-heading\&quot; wp-block-heading" id="h-key-insights">Key insights</h3>



<ul>
<li>🏓 Despite its small size, a pickleball court can accommodate four courts on a single tennis court, maximizing space and allowing for more gameplay.</li>



<li>🏓 The serving team in pickleball sets up with two players back at the baseline, while the receiving team sets up with one player back and one player up at the net, highlighting the importance of being at the net for maximum effectiveness.</li>



<li>🎾 Contrary to popular belief, players can enter the non-volley Zone before the ball bounces, allowing them to anticipate and hit the ball early.</li>



<li>🎾 You can win a rally in pickleball if your opponent commits a kitchen violation or gets hit by the ball.</li>



<li>🎾 Possession in pickleball ends when the serving team fails to win a point, resulting in a side out and a switch of serve to the other team.</li>



<li>🏅 The game always starts at zero-zero, with the exception of the second server having only one chance to serve and score points, which was implemented to minimize the advantage of the starting team.</li>



<li>⚖️ \&#8221;Anytime a possession ends, the ball is going to go over to the other side, that\&#8217;s called a side out.\&#8221;</li>
