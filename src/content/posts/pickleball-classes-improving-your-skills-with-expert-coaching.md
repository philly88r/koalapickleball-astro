<h3>Basic Rules Explanation</h3>
<p>The fundamental rules of pickleball provide a solid foundation for beginners and experienced players alike to enjoy the game&#44; focusing on court layout&#44; serving&#44; scoring&#44; and essential gameplay conventions. Understanding these rules is crucial for mastering the basics and advancing in the sport.</p>
<p>A standard pickleball court is the same size as a badminton court&#44; with dividing lines creating two service courts. The court includes a net&#44; sidelines&#44; baselines&#44; and non-volley zones &#40;also known as the &#39;kitchen&#39;&#41;.</p>
<p>The ball must be served underhand from behind the baseline&#44; diagonally to the opposing side. Only one serve attempt is allowed per server&#44; and the serve must clear the net.</p>
<p>Points are only scored by the serving team. Games are typically played to 11 points&#44; with a 2-point advantage.</p>
<p>The two-bounce rule mandates that the ball must bounce once on each side before volleying. Players must stay out of the non-volley zones unless the ball has bounced first.</p>
<p>Common faults include serves that do not clear the kitchen&#44; shots hit out of bounds&#44; and failure to let the ball bounce before volleying.</p>
