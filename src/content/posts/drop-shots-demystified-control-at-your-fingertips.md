<h2>Proper Grip and Stance</h2>
<p>Having covered the fundamentals of the drop shot&#44; I&#39;ll now explain how proper grip and stance form the foundation for executing this technique effectively.</p>
<p>I recommend using a continental grip&#44; which allows for quick adjustments and better control. Remember&#44; grip pressure is crucial&#59; maintain a relaxed hold&#44; neither too tight nor too loose. This flexibility enables precise touch and feel.</p>
<p>As for stance&#44; I suggest a balanced&#44; athletic position with your feet shoulder-width apart. Be ready to make stance adjustments as needed. Keep your knees slightly bent and your weight on the balls of your feet. This posture allows for quick movements and better shot control.</p>
<p>When preparing for a drop shot&#44; I advise shifting your weight slightly forward. This subtle change helps you generate the necessary touch while maintaining stability.</p>
