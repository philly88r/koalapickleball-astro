<p>Common causes of pop ups in pickleball can often be attributed to fundamental errors in <strong>paddle positioning</strong> and swing technique.</p>
<p>When the paddle isn&#39;t in the correct position&#44; <strong>hitting the ball</strong> too high on the paddle becomes more likely. Additionally&#44; a lack of proper <strong>follow-through technique</strong> can lead to <strong>mishits</strong> that result in pop ups.</p>
<p>It&#39;s crucial to focus on maintaining a <strong>stable stance</strong> and adjusting the paddle angle correctly at the moment of contact.</p>
<p>By ensuring that the follow-through is executed smoothly and the paddle is positioned correctly throughout the swing&#44; players can significantly reduce the occurrence of pop ups during gameplay.</p>
