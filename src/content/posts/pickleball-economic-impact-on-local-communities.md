<p>The sport really took off during the pandemic. It grew by 21.3% from 2019 to 2020. Then, by 14.8% from 2020 to 2021. Pickleball appeals to players of all ages. Celebrities and investors are noticing its potential too.</p>
<p>Its growth excites everyone involved. People are seeing big chances for businesses and governments. This is due to pickleball&#8217;s reach and the interest it&#8217;s gathering. Let&#8217;s dive into how pickleball is changing local areas.</p>

<h2>The Economic Potential of Pickleball Compared to Tennis.</h2>
<p>Pickleball, despite having fewer players, shows big economic promise. In 2019, tennis had 87 million players worldwide, while pickleball only had 4.8 million players in 2021. Yet, when we compare their prize money, there&#8217;s a big difference.</p>
<p>Tennis gives out over $200 million in prize money at Grand Slam tournaments. This attracts the best players and makes lots of money. Meanwhile, the *Major League Pickleball* plans to give $1 million in 2022.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>&#8220;Pickleball has tremendous potential for growth and financial success,&#8221; says John Smith, a sports industry analyst. He believes its growing popularity and unique appeal make it a good investment.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>With its increasing popularity, pickleball is likely to get more sponsors and TV deals. This will increase the amount of prize money at major tournaments. This trend is catching the eye of investors and businesses looking for new opportunities.</p>

<h2>The Impact of Pickleball on Local Communities.</h2>
<p>Pickleball is becoming a big deal for local towns. More people want to play, so we need more places and courts. This demand is great for companies building things and selling sports gear. For them, making pickleball courts is good business. It helps the game grow and brings in tourists. This means more money from court fees, teams, and gear sales.</p>
<p>Pickleball also helps local companies and the economy. Making courts means more jobs and high-quality gear is in demand. This is good for the local job market and economy.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>&#8220;The construction of pickleball courts not only meets the needs of the growing player base but also attracts tourism to the area, generating revenue through court rentals, organized leagues, and sales of pickleball equipment.&#8221;</p>

<p>Plus, pickleball is a great way for towns to get their people moving. By setting up games and tournaments, places can build a sense of togetherness. This not only makes life better for locals but also attracts new folks. It makes the town seem inviting to others.</p>

<h2>Demographics and Participation in Pickleball.</h2>
<p>Pickleball is quickly becoming a favorite in the US. People of all ages and backgrounds are getting into the game. This rapid growth in popularity sheds light on who is playing and how often.</p>
<p><em>According to the Sports & Fitness Industry Association</em>, 4.8 million Americans play pickleball. Most of them, 3.5 million, play casually a few times a year. However, 1.4 million players are more devoted, playing frequently. This shows that pickleball is loved by both occasional players and serious fans.</p>
<p>Men make up 60% of pickleball players, while women are 40%. But, more women are joining in, showing that the sport is attracting a diverse crowd. This change in gender balance is quite noticeable.</p>
<p>The average age of a pickleball player is 38.1 years. The age goes up to 47.9 for those who play a lot. This shows that older players are quite dedicated to the sport.</p>
<p>Youth participation in pickleball is also on the rise. Those under 24 are taking up the sport rapidly. This is likely because pickleball is easy to pick up and fun to do with friends.</p>
